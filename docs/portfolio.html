Middle Income Housing Programs: A National Scan (2025)

Description: Virginia Housing contracted with HousingForward Virginia to conduct a national scan of state-funded programs and initiatives serving households earning 80% to 120% area median income. This research provided Virginia Housing with valuable insights into state-level initiatives and high-production regions, informing future policy decisions and program implementations.

Key Contributions:

  • The first comprehensive national scan of state-level programs addressing middle-income housing needs

  • Identified 58 programs across 34 states, including three programs in Virginia

  • Programs ranged from homebuyer assistance, developer financing, and even public enterprises

Links: Report | Spreadsheet

Faith-Owned Property Ownership in Virginia (2024)

Description: The Virginia Center for Interfaith Public Policy commissioned Housing Forward Virginia to conduct a study quantifying the of land owned by faith-based organizations in Virginia. The report sought to support VICPP’s advocacy for Senate Bill 233, which aimed to grant religious organizations certain powers to overcome local zoning barriers for affordable housing development on their own land.

Technologies Used: R, QGIS

Key Contributions:

  • Determined that out of the over 4 million parcels in Virginia, 22,453 were identified as likely owned by a faith-based organization - equating to 74,116 acres

  • Identified a clear need for standardized parcel records and quality assurance among local government data collection

  • Identified zoning as a major barrier to housing development on a majority of faith-owned parcels in Northern Virginia

Links: Report | Presentation | APA Presentation
